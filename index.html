Stephen William Hawking

The man of strength who changed the concept of science

Here's a timeline of Stephen Hawking's life:

  • January 8 1942 - Born in Oxford, the eldest of four children of Franf Hawking, a biologist and Isobel Hawking, a medical research secretary.
  • October 1959 - Receives scholarship to attend University College, Oxford, where he graduates with a degree in natural science.
  • 1962 - Begins graduate research in cosmology at Cambridge University.
  • 1963 - Diagnosed with the degenerate nerve disorder ALS, or Lou Gehrig's disease.
  • 1965 - Marries his first wife, Jane Wilde, a modern languages student he met at Cambridge.
  • 1967 - His first son, Robert, is born.
  • 1970 - Jane gives birth to a daughter, Lucy.
  • 1974 - He is elected as a fellow of the Royal Society at the age of 32, one of the youngest people to receive the honour.
  • 1979 - Becomes Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambride, a prestigious position once held by Isaac Newton. Hawking holds the post until 2009. Jane gives birth to a third child, Timothy.
  • 1985 - Admitted to a hospital in Geneva with pneumonia. He survives after an operation, but loses what remained of his speech. The next year he begins communicating through an electronic voice synthesizer that gives him his trademark robotic "voice".
  • 1988 - Publishes "A Brief History of Time", a book on cosmology aimed at general public that becomes an instant bestseller.
